## Technology Integration Project Analysis

I want to highlight an initiative called [Journey Through Hallowed Ground](http://www.hallowedground.org/), a National Park Service collaboration where students created videos documenting historical site visits. The [student-produced videos](https://www.hallowedground.org/education/past-youth-education-initiatives/of-by-for-the-student) effectively bring historical narratives to life despite lacking professional production quality.

I did a parallel project at Mountain Heights Academy (a virtual high school), where geographically dispersed students researched local historical markers, learned their significance, and shared findings on a website. Here's a [sample project example](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1vQ35cDyQaNHBxFyms0zahhUZxql0s9s3BBj8FGgtm-M/edit).

## PIC-RAT Model Analysis

I applied Royce Kimmons' [PIC-RAT framework](https://edtechbooks.org/lidtfoundations/k12_tech_frameworks) to evaluate technology integration. This model uses two axes:

- **Y-axis:** Student relationship to technology (passive, interactive, creative)
- **X-axis:** Teacher tech use (replaces, amplifies, transforms)

Typing research papers about site visits is "Passive" and "Replaces." Adding interactive research elements moves higher on the continuum. Video group projects reach "Creative" and "Transforms."

This connects to David Wiley's concept of ["renewable assignments"](https://opencontent.org/blog/archives/4691) — projects that add lasting value rather than producing disposable work.
