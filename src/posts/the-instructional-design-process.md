# The Instructional Design Process

<img src="{{ '/images/posts/id-process.jpg' | url }}" alt="People collaborating on design" class="post-featured-image" />

I examined multiple instructional design models and selected the Morrison, Ross, Kemp, and Kaiman framework, positioning it within the ADDIE structure.

The model becomes more practical when applied to real situations. To enhance understanding, I developed guiding questions for each design stage — for instance, transforming "evaluation instruments" into the practical question "how will this be assessed?" These questions should be revisited throughout the design process to maintain proper focus.

I valued studying instructional design premises and included most of them in my infographic for reference. Designers risk missing their target if they lose sight of what students need to know or fail to prioritize enabling all learners to succeed.
