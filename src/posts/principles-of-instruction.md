# Principles of Instruction

<img src="{{ '/images/posts/principles-of-instruction.png' | url }}" alt="Principles of Instruction quote" class="post-featured-image" />

I found [Dr. M. David Merrill's first principles of instruction](https://edtechbooks.org/lidtfoundations/using_the_first_principles_of_instruction) compelling through personal experience. Much traditional instruction relies solely on "telling" and "asking," whereas effective curriculum incorporates "showing" and "doing" to engage learners genuinely interested in tasks.

Merrill's first principles include: activation, demonstration, application, integration, and problem-centered instruction.

## Practical Application: Creative Commons Licensing for SMEs

Here's how I applied these principles to a learning outcome about Creative Commons licensing for Subject Matter Experts:

- **Activation:** Identify prior knowledge about openly licensed materials and copyright
- **Demonstration:** Display final products and properly cited examples; show OER value
- **Application:** Have SMEs locate and correctly attribute openly licensed materials with formative feedback
- **Integration:** Share examples among SMEs; maintain a resource repository
- **Problem-centered:** Address the real-world challenge of SMEs successfully finding and attributing open educational resources

As Merrill observed: "motivation is an outcome, not a cause." Designers must intentionally plan for effective, efficient, and engaging instruction.
