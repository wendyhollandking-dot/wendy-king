# Online Course Standards

<img src="{{ '/images/posts/online-course-standards.jpg' | url }}" alt="Person working on laptop" class="post-featured-image" />

In class this week we reviewed some of the major evaluation tools, including:

- [Quality Matters Standards](https://www.qualitymatters.org/)
- [OLC OSCQR Course Design Review Scorecard](https://onlinelearningconsortium.org/consult/oscqr-course-design-review/)
- [National Standards for Quality](https://www.nsqol.org/the-standards/quality-online-courses/)
- Blackboard Exemplary Rubric
- [Universal Design for Learning](http://udlguidelines.cast.org/)

**Quality Matters** emphasizes that "tools are not used simply for their own sake." Key requirements include ensuring technology supports learning objectives, promotes engagement, provides variety, and protects student data and privacy. One novel requirement is including privacy policy links for social media and third-party tools.

**OLC OSCQR** covers similar ground while requiring skills instruction for technological components.

**National Standards for Quality** stresses accessibility and technology use for grading.

**Blackboard Exemplary Rubric** advocates technology that reduces learning labor intensity.

**Universal Design for Learning** takes a different approach, focusing on how technology addresses the "what" and "how" of learning through engagement and multiple representation methods.
