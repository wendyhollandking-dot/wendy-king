# Quality Matters Standards for Course Design

<img src="{{ '/images/posts/quality-matters.jpg' | url }}" alt="Course design workspace" class="post-featured-image" />

I have been familiar with QM for some time and have seen documentation from various faculty about specific aspects of the rubric, but walking through the complete rubric was enlightening and confirmed many of my existing practices.

The QM Higher Education Rubric (Sixth Edition) identifies eight key categories: overview and introduction, learning objectives, assessment, instructional materials, learning activities and interaction, technology, support, and accessibility. If an instructor is intentional about each of these components, it's hard to imagine it not resulting in a successful course.

My understanding of these standards has evolved throughout my career — from high school teaching focused on materials and activities, to online instruction emphasizing organization and technology, to my current work in courseware development prioritizing learning objectives, assessment, and accessibility.

For my own course design, I plan to maintain tight alignment between learning objectives, content, and assessments. I will use clear learning outcomes as a framework to ensure chosen technologies and activities teach effectively, then verify assessments measure objective achievement. Finally, I intend to view the course through the student perspective to ensure clarity, support, and accessibility.
